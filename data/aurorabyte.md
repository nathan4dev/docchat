# AuroraByte Inc.

AuroraByte Inc. is a fictional software company founded in 2014 by Maya Chen and Devon Okafor in Portland, Oregon. The company builds developer tools for distributed systems monitoring.

Its flagship product, PulseGrid, is a real-time observability platform that tracks latency, error rates, and resource usage across microservices. PulseGrid was first released publicly in March 2017 and now claims over 4,000 paying customers.

AuroraByte's engineering culture is built around three values: written communication over meetings, small autonomous teams, and a strict no-on-call-burnout policy enforced by a rotating six-person incident response pool.

The company has never raised venture funding. It has been profitable since its second year of operation, sustained entirely by subscription revenue from PulseGrid. Annual recurring revenue passed $40 million in 2023.

AuroraByte is headquartered in a converted warehouse in Portland's Central Eastside Industrial District, with a smaller remote-first engineering team distributed across North America and Europe.
