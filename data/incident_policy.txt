AuroraByte Incident Response Policy (internal reference, fictional)

Severity levels:
- SEV1: Full outage of PulseGrid's ingestion pipeline or dashboard for all customers. Must be acknowledged within 5 minutes and a status page update posted within 15 minutes.
- SEV2: Degraded performance or an outage affecting a subset of customers. Must be acknowledged within 15 minutes.
- SEV3: Minor bugs or cosmetic issues with no customer-facing impact on data accuracy. Handled during normal business hours.

The on-call rotation consists of six engineers, each serving a one-week shift. No engineer may be on call more than once every six weeks, a rule introduced in 2021 after a string of back-to-back on-call weeks led to two engineer resignations.

Every SEV1 incident requires a postmortem published within 5 business days. Postmortems are blameless by policy: they may not name individual engineers as the cause of an incident, only describe the contributing systems and process gaps.

AuroraByte's status page is hosted independently of PulseGrid's own infrastructure, specifically so that a PulseGrid outage cannot also take down the page customers check during that outage.
